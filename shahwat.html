شَهْوَة

Shahwah (Desires / Lust)

Definition

Shahwah (شهوة) means intense desire or longing, from the root (ش هـ و), referring to the soul’s inclination toward what it finds pleasurable. It is a natural human instinct, essential for preservation and lawful intimacy. However, as a sin, it refers to sexual desire that leads to unlawful thoughts, looks, or actions beyond what is permitted (halal) in Islam.

Common Practices

1. Hand Practicing (Self-Stimulation)
This is one of the most common yet hidden practices. It often starts at a young age out of curiosity, boredom, or exposure to inappropriate content, and then slowly becomes a habit.
Real-life examples:
  • Being alone at night with a phone and falling into temptation
  • Using it as a “stress relief” after a bad day
  • Doing it after watching or recalling something inappropriate
  • Treating it as something “normal” because “everyone does it.
وَٱلَّذِينَ هُمْ لِفُرُوجِهِمْ حَـٰفِظُونَ (29) إِلَّا عَلَىٰٓ أَزْوَٰجِهِمْ أَوْ مَا مَلَكَتْ أَيْمَـٰنُهُمْ فَإِنَّهُمْ غَيْرُ مَلُومِينَ (30) فَمَنِ ٱبْتَغَىٰ وَرَآءَ ذَٰلِكَ فَأُو۟لَـٰٓئِكَ هُمُ ٱلْعَادُونَ (31)

2. Watching Non-Mahram and Taking Pleasure
This is not just about looking—it’s about looking with intention and desire. The eyes become a major door to shahwah.
Real-life examples:
  • Scrolling Instagram/TikTok and pausing on attractive faces or bodies
  • Watching dramas/movies mainly for actors/actresses
  • Looking at someone in public and then looking again and again
  • Observing classmates, colleagues, or strangers with hidden desire
    👉 What starts as a glance often turns into lingering looks, and then into thoughts.
وقُل لِّلْمُؤْمِنِينَ يَغُضُّوا۟ مِنْ أَبْصَـٰرِهِمْ وَيَحْفَظُوا۟ فُرُوجَهُمْ ۚ ذَٰلِكَ أَزْكَىٰ لَهُمْ ۗ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ خَبِيرٌۢ بِمَا يَصْنَعُونَ (30)

3. Intentionally Imagining and Thinking of Opposite Gender
This is more internal but equally dangerous. Even without any physical action, a person feeds their desires through deliberate imagination.
Real-life examples:
  • Daydreaming about romantic or physical scenarios
  • Replaying past interactions or conversations for pleasure
  • Creating imaginary relationships in the mind
  • Lying in bed and entertaining such thoughts intentionally
    👉 What starts as a glance often turns into lingering looks, and then into thoughts.

Why Is It Dangerous?

Spiritual harm: It darkens the heart, weakens īmān, and reduces the sweetness of worship. A person begins to feel less guilt and more attachment to sin.
Social harm: It corrupts how a person views others—turning people into objects of desire rather than respecting them. It damages trust, weakens modesty, and spreads فساد in society.
Personal harm: It weakens self-control, increases restlessness, and creates addiction-like behavior. The heart becomes attached to pleasure, making it harder to focus, stay disciplined, or find contentment.

How to leave this sin?

  • Lower your gaze – Avoid looking at non-mahram and train your eyes to stay away from temptation. Lessen social media and avoid those videos, reels or shorts that would have even a chance of non mahram display. Don't consider it small and be trapped by it because that's how temptation initiates.
